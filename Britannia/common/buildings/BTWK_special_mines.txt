# If a mine is particularly notable, it should receive a unique mine with greater modifiers than the base "metal" mines
# This allows for a custom description also to denote why a particular mine was so significant, i.e. the tin mines in Cornwall/Devon

# Breakdown of bonuses (exc. income):
# Salt Mines: 
    # Doesn't look like salt was actually "mined" at all, but acquired by the standard process of evaporating seawater or naturall salty water
    # Can keep it in as a "mine", but localisation will need to be modified to account.
    # Salt would be used for preservation of food
    # Popular opinion bonus, & small health modifier? 
    # Small development modifier
# Copper/Tin Mines:
    # Alloy to make bronze - used to mint currency during times of inflation, decorative pieces.
    # Alloy to make pewter - used in tableware, tools
    # Copper > value than Tin I'd say. but tin is still required to smelt the alloys
    # Copper: small prestige bonus, small popular opinion 
    # Tin: small popular opinion, small construction modifier
    # I wouldn't argue these materials are particularly useful for development, but they have their places - small development modifier 
# Lead Mines:
    # Used for construction pretty much, and alloying
    # Give it a medium construction cost modifier, & good development modifier (irrigation is pretty useful, despite lead's toxicity)
# Iron Mines:
    # Obvious uses in toolmaking, weaponmaking, etc. 
    # Good local construction costs & MAA maintenance modifiers
    # Good development modifier.
# Silver Mines:
    # Silver was the standard currency for most of history, therefore we'll keep it purely as a large tax benefit w/ good development modifier (stable currency flow) 
# Gold Mines:
    # Used in the forging of regalia/church iconography/etc, not so much for currency as it isn't common. 
    # Prestige/piety bonus.
    # No development modifier
